# LOAVES AND FISHES
## A 90s JRPG-Style Biblical Parody Adventure

*"The Greatest Story Ever Played"*

### Overview
Loaves and Fishes is a PokÃ©mon-style JRPG where Jesus travels ancient Israel recruiting the 12 apostles, collecting weapon fish, and using strategic bread items in turn-based battles. It's a soft parody in the style of LEGO games - respectful, punny, and full of 90s JRPG nostalgia.

### Game Features
- **Fish Battle System**: Collect and battle with 20+ unique fish, each with their own moves, stats, and types
- **Type System**: 5 elemental types (Holy, Water, Earth, Spirit, Dark) with strategic matchups
- **12 Apostles**: Recruit all 12 apostles, each with unique battle abilities and overworld powers
- **13 Towns**: Journey through biblically-grounded locations from Nazareth to Jerusalem
- **Bread Items**: 12+ types of punny healing and buff items
- **40+ Side Quests**: Optional content with rewards
- **13 Boss Battles**: All based on scripture and historical events
- **Mini-Games**: Fishing, rhythm games, puzzles, and more
- **40 Collectible Parables**: Hidden scrolls to discover
- **New Game+**: Post-game content with super bosses

### Core Gameplay Loop
1. Arrive in town and explore
2. Visit fishmonger (buy weapon fish)
3. Visit baker (stock up on bread items)
4. Complete town challenge
5. Face the boss
6. Recruit an apostle
7. Use apostle's key power to unlock path forward
8. Travel to next town through random encounters
9. Repeat

### Tone & Style
- **Soft Parody**: Like LEGO games - poking fun, not mocking
- **90s JRPG**: Turn-based combat, pixel art, overworld exploration
- **Punny**: Fish puns, bread puns, Biblical wordplay throughout
- **Respectful**: Biblical events treated as real, humor from wordplay not mockery
- **Nostalgic**: Inspired by PokÃ©mon, Dragon Quest, Final Fantasy, Chrono Trigger

### Technical Details
- **Art Style**: 16-bit pixel art (SNES/PS1 era)
- **Genre**: JRPG / Monster Collector
- **Estimated Playtime**: 15-20 hours main story, 25-30 hours 100%
- **Platform**: PC, with potential for console/mobile ports
